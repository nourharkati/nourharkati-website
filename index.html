NOUR HARKATI
MUSIC KEXP SHOWS PRESS

Nour Harkati is a Tunisian, New York–based musician, singer-songwriter, and guembri player, bridging ancestral North African traditions with the raw pulse of contemporary urban sound. Rooted in spiritual Gnawa rhythms and shaped by eight transformative years in New York City, his music moves between meditation and movement, where philosophical lyricism meets an instinctive sense of groove.

His latest album, Moulena, released December 11, 2024, is a bold, genre-defying statement — the ancient resonance of the guembri colliding with gritty NYC drums, electronic textures, and modern songcraft. A tribute to migration, memory, and belonging, the album premiered on KEXP and was celebrated with a release concert at Public Records in New York.

Harkati’s work has carried him across continents and stages, including GlobalFEST at Lincoln Center, Celebrate Brooklyn Festival, and Habibi Festival. Following a West Coast tour and a live KEXP session released January 2, he continues into 2026 with a month-long residency at Pioneer Works — forging a singular musical language that honors lineage while pushing it forward.
